Yes, you **do not need** to master 100% of all arithmetic sub-topics in-depth to move straight up to Algebra and Calculus. Some sections are much more important (high impact), while others can be learned minimally or even postponed.

### Ranking of Arithmetic Sub-Topics Based on Impact on Algebra & Calculus

| Priority | Sub-Topic                           | Impact Level   | Main Reason |
|----------|-------------------------------------|----------------|-------------|
| **1**    | Fraction Operations                 | Very High      | The most crucial. Algebra & Calculus are full of complex fractions. |
| **2**    | Integer & Negative Number Operations| Very High      | The foundation of all algebraic manipulation. |
| **3**    | Order of Operations (PEMDAS/BODMAS) | Very High      | A single mistake makes the entire answer wrong. |
| **4**    | Exponents (Powers) & Roots          | High           | Appears constantly in algebra and calculus. |
| **5**    | GCF (Greatest Common Factor) & Simplification | High | Helps with factoring & simplification. |
| **6**    | Prime Factorization                 | Medium-High    | Foundation for factoring polynomials. |
| **7**    | LCM (Least Common Multiple)         | Medium         | Less frequently used in calculus. |
| **8**    | Divisibility Rules                  | Low-Medium     | Useful but can be skipped for now. |

### Specific Roadmap (Focus on High-Impact Only)

**Time target:** 2–4 weeks (with consistent study of 1–1.5 hours/day)

#### **Phase 1: Core Foundation (Week 1)**
Focus here first before anything else.

1. **Integer & Negative Number Operations** (2–3 days)
   - Add, subtract, multiply, and divide negative numbers.
   - Absolute value.
   - Practice: plenty of mixed positive-negative problems.

2. **Order of Operations (PEMDAS)** (1–2 days)
   - Master parentheses, exponents, multiplication-division, and addition-subtraction.
   - Practice long and complex expressions.

3. **Fractions – The King** (4–5 days, most time spent)
   - Addition & subtraction (finding common denominators).
   - Multiplication & division of fractions.
   - Mixed numbers ↔ improper fractions.
   - Simplifying fractions (using GCF).
   - Comparing fractions.
   - Conversion to decimals & percentages (basics only).

#### **Phase 2: The Bridge to Algebra (Week 2)**
4. **Exponents & Simple Roots** (3 days)
   - Power rules: `a^m × a^n = a^{m+n}`, `(a^m)^n = a^{mn}`, etc.
   - Square & cube roots.
   - Negative & fractional exponents.

5. **GCF + Prime Factorization** (3–4 days)
   - Euclidean method for GCF.
   - Prime factorization (tree method).
   - Simplifying radicals (√18 = 3√2).

#### **Phase 3: Strengthening & Transition** (Weeks 3–4)
- Practice **mixed operations** combining fractions + exponents + negative numbers.
- Start with simple **word problems** (translating sentences into mathematical expressions).
- Work on **pre-algebra** problems involving heavy expression manipulation.

Once this is complete, you are well-prepared to enter **Algebra 1** (linear equations, systems of equations, simple factoring, quadratics, etc.).

### What Can Be Postponed / Minimally Learned?

- Full divisibility rules (knowing 2, 3, 5, 9 is enough).
- LCM (just understand the basic concept + the formula LCM × GCF = a×b).
- Advanced prime numbers, strange number patterns, etc.
- Long decimal operations (you can use a calculator if absolutely necessary).

### Practical Daily Roadmap Advice

- **Days 1–10**: Focus on Phase 1 (Fractions must be 90%+ mastered).
- Solve **at least 30–40 problems** every day (do not just read the theory).
- Use resources: Khan Academy (great sequencing) or middle/high school math workbooks.
- Test yourself: If you can solve an expression like  
  `3/4 + (2/5 × 1/2) - 7/10` quickly and correctly, you are strong enough.

**Brutal conclusion:**  
If you master **Phase 1 + Phase 2** well, you will learn Algebra much more smoothly than someone who studies all of arithmetic carelessly. Fractions are the biggest bottleneck.
